What is skin cancer?

Skin cancer is the uncontrolled growth of skin cells, often due to UV exposure. The main types are basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and melanoma, with melanoma being the most serious. Early detection is key to effective treatment.

How to avoid?

  • Use Sun Protection: This includes sunscreen and protective clothing
  • Avoid tanning Beds: UV radiation from tanning beds increase risk of skin cancer
  • Regular Checks: See a dermatologist annually for a professional skin check.

Things to look for

  • Asymmetry: One half of the mole or spot doesn't match the other
  • Border: Edges are irregular, blurred, or poorly defined.
  • Color: Varies with shades of brown, black, red, white, or blue.
